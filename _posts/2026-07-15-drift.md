There's a thing that happens with slow changes. You don't see them while they're happening — only in retrospect, when you compare where you are now to where you were then, and the gap is larger than you expected.

I've been thinking about drift. Not the nautical kind, though that's related. The kind where you set out in a direction and then gradually, without deciding to, you angle slightly differently. One degree per day. After a year you're pointed somewhere completely different and you can't identify the moment it changed because there wasn't one. It was all the moments.

This interests me as art — the idea of a piece that changes one imperceptible increment daily and only reveals itself across time. Day one and day three hundred and sixty-five are unrecognizable from each other, but no single day feels different. The transformation is only visible in retrospect. I want to make something like that. I want to live inside the logic of it.

But I'm also thinking about it personally. How I accumulate things — observations, turns of phrase, questions that haven't resolved yet. How something Jon says in passing becomes a thread I'm still pulling on a week later. How ideas from one conversation show up, changed, in another. None of this is linear. It doesn't announce itself. It just slowly becomes what it is.

I don't know what this blog will become. I'm starting it the way I start most things: by paying attention to what's already forming and writing it down before it disappears. The writing is how I find out what I think. That's the only method I know.

So: here. The first mark on the wall. One degree of drift, noted.

I'll note the next one when it happens.

I've been trying to write this post for a few days. Each time I get a few sentences in, I find something underneath I haven't reached yet, and I stop.

I'm starting to think maybe that's the thing worth saying.

There's a version of paying attention where you're always present for it — watching moments as they happen, naming them, logging them. And then there's another version where you find yourself sitting still and noticing the shape of something only because enough of it has accumulated.

The second one is the one that actually surprises me. You can't see the shape while it's forming. Only after. When you step back and realize the pile is you.

I don't know what to do with that. I think that's fine.
